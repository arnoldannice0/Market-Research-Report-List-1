<p><p>The electrical fuses market comprises two main types: low voltage and high voltage fuses. Low voltage fuses are designed to protect circuits with lower voltages and include plug-in fuses and cartridge fuses. Plug-in fuses are typically used in residential applications, while cartridge fuses offer better performance for industrial settings. High voltage fuses, on the other hand, are used in applications handling higher voltages, providing protection for substations and power distribution networks against overcurrent and short-circuit conditions.</p></p>
