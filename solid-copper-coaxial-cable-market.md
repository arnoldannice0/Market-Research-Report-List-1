<p><p>The solid copper coaxial cable market is primarily divided into two types: baseband coaxial cable and broadband coaxial cable. Baseband coaxial cable is designed for short-distance communications, typically used in local area networks (LANs) and video transmission, transmitting high-frequency signals without modulation. In contrast, broadband coaxial cable supports a wider frequency range, enabling multiple channels of data, video, and voice transmissions over longer distances. This versatility makes it ideal for cable television and internet services, accommodating high-bandwidth applications.</p></p>
